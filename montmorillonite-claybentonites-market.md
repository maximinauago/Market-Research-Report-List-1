<p><p>Montmorillonite clay, commonly known as bentonite, is categorized into three main types based on its dominant cation: sodium, calcium, and potassium bentonite. Sodium bentonite is valued for its high swelling capacity and water retention, making it ideal for drilling fluids and sealing applications. Calcium bentonite has lower swelling properties, often used as an absorbent or in agriculture. Potassium bentonite, while less common, is primarily used in fertilizers and other applications where potassium is essential. Each type serves unique industrial and agricultural needs.</p></p>
