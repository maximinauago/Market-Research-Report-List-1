<p><p>The battery energy storage market consists of several key types, primarily including lithium-ion (Li-ion), lead-acid, and sodium-sulfur batteries. Li-ion batteries are favored for their high energy density, efficiency, and long cycle life, making them ideal for renewable energy integration and electric vehicles. Lead-acid batteries are cost-effective and widely used for stationary applications, though they have lower energy density and lifespan. Sodium-sulfur batteries offer high energy density and thermal stability, suitable for large-scale storage but are less common due to cost and temperature requirements.</p></p>
